About

He is currently a lecturer at SocTrang Community College in Vietnam and a member of the Engineering and Technology Faculty from October 2011 to the present.

From September 2019 until now, He is a Ph.D. student at the CRIL laboratory, Artois University, studying with the thesis title "Belief Merging for improving ontologies and open-domain knowledge exploitation" under the supervision of Professor Sébastien Konieczny, Associate Professor Ivan Varzinczak, and Associate Professor Zied Bouraoui.

His research interests mainly focus on machine learning, data mining, and high-performance computing in computer vision, particularly videos/image classification through state-of-the-art algorithms and deep learning methods. At present, his concentration concerns Knowledge Representation and Reasoning, Description Logics, and flexible methods for reasoning with ontologies. Recently, his research topic is directing to Belief Merging, Qualitative Spatial Calculi, Natural Language Processing. Learn More
